Job Opportunities

After graduating from a Humanities or Social Sciences course abroad, you may pick from the following career paths that most HUMSS students opt for:

Politician

a person experienced in the art or science of government; especially : one actively engaged in conducting the business of a government.

Journalist

a person engaged in journalism; especially : a writer or editor for a news medium.

Policeman

A person responsible for the prevention and detection of crime and the maintenance of public order

Criminologist

A criminologist is a professional who studies crime to prevent and reduce it from occurring

News Anchor

A person working for television and radio networks and local stations, they introduce reporters' videotaped and live reports, analyze and select stories, and interview guests.

Consultant

provides expert advice, guidance, and solutions to individuals, organizations, or businesses seeking assistance in specific areas.

Archaeologist

a person who studies human history and prehistory through the excavation of sites and the analysis of artifacts and other physical remains

Historian

are scholars who study and interpret the past, examining historical records, events, and developments to understand and explain human societies' evolution over time.

Geologist

a scientist who studies the solid, liquid, and gaseous matter that constitutes Earth and other terrestrial planets, as well as the processes that shape them

Linguistic

A person that explores the nature, structure, and use of language and communication, both within and across languages and cultures.

Museum Curator

a person who works in a museum and is responsible for curating collections, exhibits and other artifacts that will be displayed at the museum.

Oceanographer

ocean scientists who study the physical, chemical, and biological features of the ocean and all its complex relationships with the planet.

Weather Forecaster

A person responsible for analyzing weather data and formulating weather reports

Political Scientist

A political scientist leads a career that is deeply invested in the understanding of both the theories and applications of the policies and laws that affect governmental systems, citizens and the economy

Economist

an expert who studies the relationship between a society's resources and its production or output.

Sociologist

study society and social behavior by examining the groups, cultures, organizations, social institutions, and processes that develop when people interact and work together.
